Abstract

The UK Biobank is an extremely large dataset of healthcare information relating to half a million volunteer participants, thanks to a project where many individuals from the UK agreed to be studied for 30 years. The dataset also has many fields, ranging from blood samples to lifestyle. The data is accessible for health-care related researchers, after making a formal request.

Long description

UK Biobank

Overview

UK Biobank is a large-scale, long-term prospective cohort study collecting biological and health-related data from around 500,000 participants across the United Kingdom.

Participants aged 40 to 69 years were recruited between 2006 and 2010 and consented to share their health data and be followed for at least 30 years. The project’s aim is to support research into the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of disease across biomedical and population health fields.

Nature has described UK Biobank as an “unprecedented open access database.”

Data Collected

UK Biobank holds more than 10,000 variables on its participants, covering a wide range of data types:

  • Biological samples (blood, urine, saliva)
  • Physical and physiological measurements (height, weight, blood pressure, bone density, lung capacity)
  • Imaging data (body, brain, bone, and eye scans)
  • Activity tracking and lifestyle questionnaire data
  • Genetic and proteomic information

More than 15 million biological samples are stored, and the online database exceeds 30 petabytes of data. UK Biobank’s genome sequencing, proteomic, and imaging datasets are among the largest in the world.

Study Design

Each participant visited an assessment centre where they:

  • Completed questionnaires on lifestyle, medical history, and diet
  • Underwent physical measurements
  • Provided blood and urine samples for long-term storage and DNA extraction

All participants’ data are linked to their electronic health records through the UK National Health Service (NHS). This enables long-term tracking of disease events, prescriptions, and mortality.

Researchers do not have direct access to participants, and all records are strictly anonymised. Feedback to participants is limited to basic measures (e.g., BMI, blood pressure). Other findings, such as genetic risks, are not disclosed to participants or their doctors to protect privacy and prevent discrimination.

Access for Researchers

Since 2012, approved researchers worldwide have been able to apply for access to UK Biobank data for health-related research that serves the public interest.

A typical study compares groups of participants who developed a particular condition (e.g., cancer, heart disease, diabetes, Alzheimer’s) with those who did not, to examine how genes, lifestyles, and medications interact to influence disease risk.

By 2017, UK Biobank had released genetic information for analysis. At that time, participants had collectively recorded approximately 1.3 million hospitalisations, 40,000 cancer diagnoses, and 14,000 deaths.

Methodology

The volunteers answer questionnaires and do visits in which various samples are collected. The methodology varies based on the data being collected, but a summary page is found here: https://www.ukbiobank.ac.uk/about-our-data/our-participants/

Usage instructions: There are some caveats relating to how the data should be used responsibly. The reasoning given when applying to receive the data should be maintained, and the data itself should not be shared and it should be stored in secure places. The data is anonymised, and you are not allowed to attempt to re-identify participants. When you obtain data, you need to send it (along with code) to BioBank within 6 or 12 months. There are annual reports to be made, relating to how the data is used. The research produced should be ethically sound, and any publications are to be notified to biobank.
